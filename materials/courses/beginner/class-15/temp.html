(+) We have coffee for breakfast.

(-) We don't have tea for breakfast.


Simple present (+) and (-): I, you, we, they


• We use the simple present to talk about:


present habits (=things we usually do): I have coffee for breakfast.

things that are always true: In my country, we eat a lot of rice.


• Simple present is the same for I, you (singular and plural), we, and they.


• We make (-) sentences with don't, e.g., We don't have coffee.
WRONG: We not have coffee.
