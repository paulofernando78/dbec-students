Grammar (presentation)


Meaning + Pronunciation + Form


FUTURE FORMS


be going to + base form


1.16

future plans and intentions


My sister's going to adopt a child.

Are you going to buy a new car or a used one?

I'm not going to go to New York City tomorrow. The meeting is canceled.


• We use be going to when we have already decided to do something.


1.17

predictions


The Yankees are going to win. They're playing really well.

Look at those black clouds. I think it's gonna rain.


• We also use be going to to make a prediction about the future, especially when we have some evidence (e.g., we can see black clouds).

e.g. = for example (from latim 'exempli gratia')


present continuous: be + verb + -ing


1.18

future arrangements


Lorna and James are getting married in October.

We're meeting at 10:00 tomorrow in Jack's office.

Jane's leaving on Friday and coming back next Tuesday.


• We often use the present continuous for future arrangements.

• There is a very little difference between the present continuous and be going to for future plans / arrangements, and often you can use either.

- be going to shows that you have made a decision.

We're going to get married next year.

- the present continous emphasizes that you have made the arrangements.

We're getting married on October 12th (twelfth)

* We've already ordered the invitations, etc.

Já encomendamos os convites, etc.


1.19



will + base form


instant decisions, promises, offers, predictions, future facts

I'll have the steak. (instant decision)

I won't tell anybody where you are. (promise)

I'll carry that bag for you. (offer)

You'll love New York City! (prediction)

I'll be home all afternoon. (future fact)

Grandma: So what are you going to do next year? Adam? Are you going to go to college? Adam? Can you hear me?

Grandson: Sorry, Grandma. What did you say?

Grandma: I said, are you going to go to college next year?

Grandson: No, Grandma. I've already told you like 100 times. I'm' not going to go to college. I'm going to look for a job. I need to earn some money.

Grandma: All right, dear. You don't have to shout. I can hear you perfectly well, thank you. What time is it?

Grandson: Ten to three. I'll get you some coffee.

Grandma: Oh, thank you. That'd be nice.

Daughter: Bye!
