Elementary Grammar

Unit 37 There is / There are



There to be Haver, existir


A


Take a look at the singular form:


❗There's = There is Há, existe


AFFIRMATIVE

There's a girl on the roof.

There's a cat at the window.



There's a train at 10:30.

There's a big tree in the garden.

There's nothing interesting on TV. Turn it off.


Dialogue:

A: Do you have any money?

B: Yes, There's some in my wallet.


NEGATIVE


❗There isn't = There is not Não há, não existe


There isn't a man on the roof. It's a cat.

There isn't a train at the station. It's gone. Já foi embora.

This gardem is huge, but there isn't a tree with a good shade sombra.

There isn't a cat at the window. It's a bid. Can you see it?


INTERROGATIVE


Is there a man on the roof?

Is there a train at 9a.m.?

Is there a garden in this house?

Is there a cat at the window?

Now take a look at the plural form:


AFFIRMATIVE


There are some big trees in the garden now.

There are a lot of accidents on this road.

There are 7 days in a week. Sunday, Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, and Saturday.


NEGATIVE


This restaurant is very quiet. There aren't many people here.


INTERROGATIVE


How many players are there in a soccer team? There are 11 players.


Dialogue:

A: Are there any restaurants near here?

B: Yes, there are. / No, there aren't.


B
